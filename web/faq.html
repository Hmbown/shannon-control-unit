Frequently Asked Questions

Understanding Shannon's approach to information-theoretic anomaly detection

"Why use NCD instead of Deep Learning?"

It's complementary, not a replacement. NCD offers advantages where DL struggles: zero training data, high speed, privacy, and interpretability. We emphasize the Hybrid ROI model (prefiltering for DL) and Privacy-first use cases.

"What about DDoS? Isolation Forest (IF) seems better."

Correct, IF is often stronger for purely volumetric attacks as it detects density changes easily. Shannon excels at structural complexity. We recommend a hybrid/ensemble approach (IF + Shannon) for comprehensive coverage.

"Performance depends heavily on settings (compressor/window). How do I choose?"

This is a known limitation, and the optimal configuration is dataset-dependent. We provide guidance based on data characteristics and are developing "Auto-Tuning" for the Enterprise version. We also publish detailed configuration guides and ablation studies for open-source users.

"The F1 scores (e.g., 0.330) seem low."

Unsupervised detection on highly imbalanced data is challenging. These scores are competitive among baselines and are achieved without any training data. We focus on AUPRC (which is better for imbalance) and highlight the unsupervised advantage of zero-training novelty detection.

"Isn't NCD just checking `gzip` ratios?"

No. NCD uses compressors but is a mathematically rigorous metric that normalizes the result based on the complexity of the objects being compared. It's more robust than simple compression ratios. We provide documentation detailing the NCD formula and the importance of normalization.

"What is the Shannon Control Unit (SCU)?"

The SCU is our research into automatic capacity control for neural networks. It's a training methodology that aims to maintain precise information capacity during LLM training, potentially eliminating manual hyperparameter tuning. Think of it as a "transistor for AI" - providing reliable, automatic control of information flow. Currently in early research phase.

"Are you building your own LLMs?"

We're not building new foundation models from scratch. Instead, we're researching training methodologies that could improve how any LLM is trained. The SCU approach adds information-theoretic principles to existing architectures (like Qwen, Mistral, or Llama) to potentially achieve better compression and more efficient training.

"How does SCU relate to your NCD work?"

Both use compression as a fundamental measure. NCD uses compression distance for anomaly detection without training. SCU uses compression targets to guide neural network training. They're complementary approaches: NCD for zero-shot detection, SCU for more intentional model training. Both stem from information theory and Kolmogorov complexity.

"When will SCU models be available?"

The SCU is currently in research phase. We're developing and validating the training methodology on smaller models (1B-3B parameters) before scaling up. Our focus is on proving the approach works reliably before releasing anything. Check our GitHub for research updates and potential early access to experimental models.
